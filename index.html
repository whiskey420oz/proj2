Visual Arts

Visual arts encompass diverse creative expressions, including painting, sculpture, and photography, conveying emotions and ideas through visual means.

.

Mona Lisa

This iconic portrait, completed between 1503 and 1519, features the enigmatic smile of Lisa del Giocondo. It is housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris and is renowned for its sophisticated use of sfumato and composition, making it a masterpiece of the Renaissance period.

The Last Supper

This mural, created between 1495 and 1498, depicts the moment Jesus announces that one of his disciples will betray him. Located in Milan's Santa Maria delle Grazie, it is one of the most studied artworks in history.

The Birth of Venus

Created around 1486, this painting depicts Venus emerging from the sea on a shell. It embodies the ideals of beauty and love during the Renaissance and is housed in the Uffizi Gallery, Florence.

Guernica

Created in response to the bombing of Guernica during the Spanish Civil War, this powerful mural conveys the horrors of war through its chaotic composition and monochromatic palette. It is housed in the Museo Reina Sofia, Madrid.

The Girl with a Pearl Earring

Often referred to as the "Mona Lisa of the North," this 17th-century painting features a young girl wearing an exotic dress and a large pearl earring, capturing a moment of intimate gaze. It resides in the Mauritshuis, The Hague.

The Starry Night

Painted in 1889, this swirling night sky over a small town reflects Van Gogh's emotional turmoil and unique style. It is displayed at the Museum of Modern Art in New York and symbolizes the artist's deep connection to nature.

The Creation of Adam

Part of the Sistine Chapel ceiling, this fresco illustrates God giving life to Adam. The nearly touching fingers of God and Adam have become a universal symbol of divine creation and human potential.

The Night Watch

This large group portrait, painted in 1642, showcases Rembrandt's mastery of light and shadow. It captures a moment of action among a militia company and is displayed at the Rijksmuseum in Amsterdam.

The Persistence of Memory

This surreal painting from 1931 features melting clocks draped over a landscape, symbolizing the fluidity of time and reality. It is part of the collection at the Museum of Modern Art in New York

American Gothic

Painted in 1930, this iconic image represents rural American values during the Great Depression through its portrayal of a farmer and his daughter standing stoically before their home. It is displayed at the Art Institute of Chicago.

Literary Arts

Literary arts embrace a wide range of creative expressions, such as poetry, fiction, and drama, capturing emotions and ideas through the beauty of written and spoken language.

.

Pride and Prejudice

Published in 1813, this novel explores themes of love, class, and reputation through the story of Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates societal expectations while seeking true love.

The Great Gatsby

Set during the Roaring Twenties, this novel critiques the American Dream through Jay Gatsby's tragic pursuit of wealth and love amidst moral decay.

One Hundred Years of Solitude

This landmark novel chronicles several generations of the Buendía family in Macondo while weaving themes like solitude and magical realism into their lives.

Brave New World

This dystopian narrative published in1932 critiques consumerism by depicting an engineered society where happiness is enforced through technology—raising questions about individuality versus conformity.

The Odyssey

An ancient Greek epic poem attributed to Homer narrating Odysseus’s journey home after Trojan War filled with encounters reflecting heroismand adventure—serving as foundational literature influencing storytelling throughout history.

Moby-Dick

This epic tale published in 1851 follows Captain Ahab's obsessive quest for revenge against Moby Dick, a giant whale that symbolizes nature's power over humanity.

1984

Published in 1949, this dystopian novel portrays a totalitarian regime that uses surveillance to control citizens' thoughts and actions—a chilling exploration of freedom versus oppression.

Wuthering Heights

Released posthumously in1847,this novel explores passionate yet destructive love between Heathcliffand Catherine Earnshaw against Yorkshire’s moors—showcasing gothic elements intertwined with human emotion.
